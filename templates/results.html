Understanding the Metrics

Accuracy: The percentage of correct predictions out of all predictions. {{ "%.2f"|format(data.results.accuracy * 100) }}% means {{ "%.0f"|format(data.results.accuracy * 100) }} out of 100 predictions were correct.

F1-Score: The harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing a balance between the two. A score closer to 1.0 indicates better performance.

Precision: Of all positive predictions, how many were actually positive? Higher precision means lower false positive rate.

Simple Explanations for Normal Users
  • Accuracy: Think of it as a grade out of 100. If accuracy is 85%, it means the model got 85 out of 100 predictions right.
  • Precision: When the model says something is positive (like "this review is good"), precision tells you how often it's right. If precision is 90%, then 9 out of 10 times the model says something is good, it really is good.
  • Recall: Out of all the truly good reviews, recall tells you what percentage the model correctly identified. If recall is 80%, it means the model found 80% of all the actually good reviews.
  • F1-Score: A single number that balances precision and recall. It's like getting one score that considers both how careful the model is (precision) and how thorough it is (recall).

Understanding the Classification Report
  • Precision: Of all positive predictions, how many were actually positive? High precision means low false positive rate.
  • Recall: Of all actual positives, how many were correctly predicted? High recall means low false negative rate.
  • F1-Score: The harmonic mean of precision and recall. Balances both metrics.
  • Support: The number of actual occurrences of the class in the dataset.

The macro avg calculates metrics for each class independently and takes the average, treating all classes equally regardless of their frequency.

The weighted avg calculates metrics for each class and takes the average weighted by support (the number of true instances for each class), accounting for class imbalance.

Simple Explanations for Normal Users
  • Class 0 (Negative): Represents negative reviews (bad movies, products, etc.)

  • If Class 1 has high recall (e.g., 0.85), it means the model catches 85% of all actually positive reviews.

Understanding Model Performance

Accuracy: The percentage of correct predictions. Higher is better, but can be misleading with imbalanced datasets.

Weighted Average Metrics: These metrics are calculated considering the support (number of samples) for each class, making them more reliable for imbalanced datasets.

  • Precision: Of all positive predictions, how many were actually positive?
  • Recall: Of all actual positives, how many were correctly predicted?
  • F1-Score: Harmonic mean of precision and recall, balancing both metrics.

To compare models, look for higher values in all metrics. Models with consistently high scores across all metrics are generally preferred.

Simple Explanations for Normal Users
  • Accuracy: Like a test score - if accuracy is 90%, the model gets 90 out of 100 predictions right.
  • Precision: When the model predicts something belongs to a category, precision tells you how often it's correct. High precision means fewer false alarms.
  • Recall: Out of all items that truly belong to a category, recall tells you what percentage the model found. High recall means the model misses fewer items.
  • F1-Score: A balanced score that considers both precision and recall. It's especially useful when you care about both finding most items AND being accurate about what you find.
